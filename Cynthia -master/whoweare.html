Who We Are

A non-profit association with a distinctive identity. At 1-to-1, we connect companies with volunteers who want to help homeless people. We are driven by a single goal: to do our part in making the world a better place for all. We strive to build productive relationships and make a positive impact on the world.

Our Team

Pauline Baron

Founder of 1 to 1

Originally from France, Pauline is spending her last year of studies abroad, in UC Berkeley’s Haas Business School. “Every time I walk by homeless people in the street, the same thing happens: I feel very sorry for them but I keep my eyes off, I want to help them but at the same time I feel useless, I want to stop and talk but instead I just carry on… I was tired of just feeling sorry. When I arrived in the Bay Area last August, I was shocked by the number of people living on the street. I decided to stop being a spectator and act. As one of my teachers told me once: When we want something, most of the time we can achieve it”

Gabrielle

Co-Founder of 1 to 1

Gabrielle is also a student at UC Berkeley’s Haas School of Business. She is enrolled in the same program as Pauline and they met each other during their first year in EDHEC Business School (France). “What struck me when I arrived in the Bay was the tremendous and very visible inequality among people in the street. Where I lived in France, many homeless people are also wandering but here, in the Bay, I first thought poverty was so endemic that nothing could help it, and that on an individual level nothing could be done to improve their lives. When we had the chance to create a project with 42 students, it was clear I wanted to do something for these people.”

Cynthia Luu

Co-Founder of 1 to 1

Cynthia currently studies at 42 school, a reowned coding school in the Silicon Valley of which she is an ambassador. She has a background in Graphic and UI/UX Design and project management. She highly contributed to making our 1-to-1 website become reality. “I am passionate about 1-to-1. I want our non-profit association to succeed in helping more homeless people.”
