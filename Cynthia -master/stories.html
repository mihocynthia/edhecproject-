Their Stories

1-to-1 was able to go deeper and find more stories about the homeless. We were also able to gather some stats. Among the Berkeley population, there are 50% with no education, 73% drug or alcohol use, 36% mental health problems, and 85% are local.

Michael, Shattuck Avenue

Originally from Chicago, Illinois, he left his family and crossed the United States by bus to find a job. More than 2,000 miles away from his homeland, he used to work in restaurants, cleaning dishes. Sometimes he would work as a waiter. But after having four or five jobs, he decided to quit. He ended up living on the street. Three months have passed. He’s still left with no money.

Godwin, Dwight Way Shelter

Godwin works in several shelters in Berkeley including the Dwight Way Shelter & the Harrison House. He obtained his green card in 1999. Since then, he’s always been working in the social field. It has been 21 years now. Godwin had hard times when he was younger. He had to flee his own country because of the civil war. It helped him a lot to help other people, to listen to them and make them happier. It kept him busy and prevented him from reflecting on his own traumas. Godwin told us about a homeless man he used to know before facing health issues. Now he’s in a wheelchair and can’t stand anymore. It makes him sad to see how bad people's lives can become. He also mentioned that recently, a Mexican family arrived in Harrison House with two children. To him, children are just innocent people that don’t deserve to face those situations.
